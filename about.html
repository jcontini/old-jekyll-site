In April 2015, I moved to Jakarta to work as the ICT lead for an Australian aid program that focuses on improving service delivery and economic opportunities for Indonesians.

Before that, I lived in Boston and DC doing technology consulting for US government health agencies (HHS) and USAID programs. Through the latter, I had the opportunity to work with health ministries in Africa and the Caribbean, and gained an appreciation for building ICT solutions in low-connectivity settings.

I'm a big fan of the Principles for Digital Development, and think that the ICT4D scene can learn a lot from digital service agencies like those in the US, UK, and Australia.

My free time is filled with reading, exploring Asia, nerding out, and hanging out with good people. My current interests are renewable energy, behavioral psychology, sustainable social enterprises, online education, web development, data vizualization, and geneology.

Referring to myself in the third person isn't my thing, but some people ask for it, so here's your copy & paste bit if you're looking for it:

Joe Contini is the ICT lead for KOMPAK, an Australian aid program focusing on improving public service delivery and economic opportunities for the poorest Indonesians. Prior to moving to Jakarta in 2015, Joe spent 4.5 years at Abt Associates providing technology consulting services to US health agencies and USAID programs while based in Boston and Washington DC. His experience includes product management, technology strategy, business analysis, web application development, systems interoperability, SMS/IVR data collection, web usability & accessibility (section 508, WCAG 2.0), web analytics & optimization, and implementing collaboration platforms. He has experience providing services to federal and private clients in Asia, Africa, Australia, the United States, and Latin America.
